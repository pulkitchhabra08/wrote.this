intro

okay so. I wrote some poems.

not because I planned to — they kept showing up while I was supposed to be doing other things. studying, mostly. occasionally staring at the ceiling. once while watching a bird outside my window for way too long.

they're about having a lot of feelings and not always knowing what to do with them. the chaos of wanting too much. a stranger who made me smile for no reason. love showing up mid-sentence like it owns the place.

pretty normal stuff, honestly.

I put them here because they felt too big for my notes app. read them in order or don't — either works. hope something hits.

— Pulkit Chhabra

poem i  ·  the rush
The Speed of Things
life said "keep up" and I said "wait what"

wrote this on a day when everything felt like it was moving and I was somehow standing still. headphones in, world on mute. you know the feeling.

Life's moving fast, I feel my heart stuck in slow-mo. All the things that once made sense — now I'm like… wait, was that a no-go? Things that felt simple and fun now chase me like I owe them money in this run. A boy finds peace in songs, sings along just to feel he belongs. Headphones in, world on mute, low battery — but still kind of cute. Reality hits. No warning. No crown. Just shows up one day and sits you down. The leaves have slowly started to brown, even the sky feels a little less town. Life moves on, hands out wings, says "fly high." But sometimes it's fine to just sit… and cry. And somehow, between the chaos and the plea, life starts making a little sense to me — not perfect, not whole, not fully free… but enough to finally just… be.
the headphones thing

headphones in, world on mute — that's not escapism, that's survival. three-minute songs where everything feels manageable. we all have a version of this.

on one line

honestly the most me line in the whole collection. running on empty but still showing up. that's the vibe.

poem ii  ·  who am i
Diamond and Dust
my mom's softness, my dad's sharpness, and me somewhere in between

this one got personal fast. I didn't plan to write about my parents but here we are. the "orphan answers" line is my favourite thing I've ever written, not gonna lie.

I found a life — almost here, almost whole, just cooking under the fire of ambitions. There are questions unanswered. There are days in trouble, feels like I'm living in a bubble on the verge of bursting. It's made of diamond crystals, baked under heat and pressure. I'm soft inside, with emotions from my mother, and sharp outside, shaped by my father's wisdom. Life sometimes gets boring — but maybe that's fine. His ambitions are high, he believes he can fly. The sky felt low, so he aimed for space. Fascinated by the mysteries of the universe, he had some questions and some orphan answers — so he just put it all in a verse.
orphan answers

answers that exist but don't have a question yet. or questions that lost their answer somewhere. I think that's just what it feels like to be curious and unfinished.

why "he" halfway through

switched to "he" because "I" felt too close. sometimes you need a little distance to look at yourself honestly.

poem iii  ·  winning
The Boy at the Top
what if you win and it feels weird

started as a poem about ambition. ended up being about the strange silence when you get what you wanted. the "well played" ending kind of ruined me.

One… Two… Three… Go. The race began with a blasting blow — every face wore a divine glow. Tiny feet with hopes so stunning, innocent minds… yet already running. Years rolled by, the crowd grew wide, big fat lies ran side by side. Dreams were loud, the truth was humming — still they never stopped their running. Then the road split — he found two ways, and he had dreams to chase. He paused a moment, his courage numbing, he cried… he tried… then stopped becoming. But he made a choice and rose again — not out of pressure, not for acclaim. He walked his path in his own sure way, and Shakespeare echoed loud that day — "to unpathed waters, undreamed shores" — and he knew he wasn't running chores. He wasn't racing for crowns or scores. He was running to become… something more. And then he was there — the long-dreamed top, yet silence hit harder than any drop. Maybe he'd won. Maybe he was done. But he felt he'd lost the boy he'd begun. He stood alone in the thinning air, realized life was never fair. He was amazed. He had changed. And everyone just said — "well played."
well played

two words. everyone claps. no one asks if you're okay. that's the whole poem in a caption.

what was lost

the scariest part of growing up isn't failing. it's succeeding and not recognising yourself anymore.

poem iv  ·  the bird
The Bird I Watched Instead
I was supposed to be studying

literally watched a bird from my window for like 20 minutes one afternoon. wrote this instead of doing what I was supposed to. zero regrets.

The bird is alone in the open sky — not lonely, just singular. It cuts through the blue like a thought you didn't know you were having. First flight or hundredth — it doesn't matter. Every rise through soft and shining clouds is a new country. Fear visits sometimes, but adventure never leaves. The view from up there must be surreal — or maybe that's just what I tell myself looking up from down here, wondering if any of it is real, or just a beautiful hallucination I've been granted on a slow afternoon. Whatever it is — never mind. My mind has found its rhyme.
why a bird

it had nowhere to be. no deadlines. no expectations. I was jealous of a bird. that felt worth writing about.

the ending

not giving up. just — some things are beautiful and unexplainable and that's enough. you don't need to solve everything.

poem v  ·  hope
The Stranger at Seven
one person, one moment, somehow enough

this actually happened. I was having a rough day, saw someone walking by, felt weirdly better. couldn't explain it so I wrote it instead.

I was going down the lane with a heart full of pain — then I saw a candle of sane. She was a messenger from heaven, And it was almost seven. that hour when the light goes golden and everything feels like it might mean something. There was a softness in her eyes, a stillness in the way she moved — something that felt, it lifted my mood, like the world remembered how to be good. I then had a SMILE Not at her — at the proof of her. I dared to walk that extra MILE, and there was a silver-line waiting quiet, waiting patient, for its share of sunshine. She was a flame of hope — lit not to last, but to remind me there's nothing I cannot cope.
that line

she didn't do anything. she just existed, kindly, at the right moment. and that was evidence that the world still had good things in it.

almost seven

that golden hour light thing is real. everything feels like it might mean something at almost seven.

poem vi  ·  love??
What's Love? (I Wasn't Supposed to Write This)
I was literally just sitting there

I genuinely don't know how this poem happened. I was studying. then suddenly I was writing about love. mid-poem I even asked myself what was going on. kept it in.

What's love? An emotion, a notion, or just attraction lost in the chase of perfection? No one's perfect, no one's ideal — we all got scars that never fully heal. I had my flaws, my reckless laws, yet still they loved me — just because. Love is acceptance, sometimes demand. It's knowing their little "kaand." It's comfortable, it's easy. Sometimes silly, sometimes cheesy. It's being you, unapologetically true — and being the one in their point of view. It's butterflies dancing, a spark in the air, like flowers deciding to bloom everywhere. From roses to poses, to promises and pauses — those 3 a.m. calls. Those collective goals. But — wait a second… what got me here? Why am I writing about love so clear? I don't even know — just vibing, just chilling, studying, sitting… then words started spilling. What's love? Maybe it's exactly this — words that arrive before you know you're missing something.
the mid-poem crisis

the most honest moment in the collection. the poem catching itself mid-thought. I kept it in because it felt too real to cut.

words that arrive before you know you're missing something

that's love. that's also poetry. didn't plan that — it just landed that way.

poem vii  ·  the moon
The Moon Doesn't Apologize for Its Scars
the one where everything kind of makes sense

last poem. wrote it late at night. the moon was actually out. felt right to end here — not with answers, just with the moon doing its thing, scars and all.

It's me, sitting in my room, at my table, with a list so long it could stretch to the moon. Exams are near — they're loud in my ear, but somehow I'm drifting, not moving from here. They call it delulu, lost in some fantasy view, but maybe it's solulu for dreams that feel trulu. I know I belong somewhere — not here, not nowhere. There's light in the distance. I feel it out there. But books keep staring, deadlines declaring: "prepare for your future" like I'm not already caring. I say I'm fine. Every single time. Would it be my crime if I crossed that line? If I paused the race, just slowed the pace, and let my thoughts breathe in their own space? The music is on, and my worries are gone — for three-minute songs I feel kind of strong. The work feels lighter, the room feels tighter, the songs grow quieter, my thoughts softer, slighter. I think I'm missing a version of me who laughed a bit louder, who felt more free. Why do we age? Turn life to a stage? Are we getting wiser — or just writing a heavier page? Look at the moon — it's scarred, not immune, yet it shines every night like it owns the tune. And suddenly rhyme falls into line, like even my chaos is trying to be fine.
the moon

every crater is something that hit it. still shines. that's it. that's the whole thing.

my favourite line in the collection

not saying everything's fine. just saying it's trying. that distinction matters more than it sounds.

heavier page

are we getting wiser or just accumulating more weight. I still don't know. asking felt more honest than answering.
